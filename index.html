    Quadrant Framework for Workforce Value Mapping
    Reference guide: how the 12 values roll up into 4 leadership diagnostic quadrants.
    ⌄

    This model translates frontline sentiment into actionable leadership intelligence by consolidating the 12 workplace values into four quadrants for clearer interpretation, heat-mapping, and longitudinal tracking.

    🧭 Quadrant I — Foundational Security
    “Am I materially safe and supported enough to do my job?”
    • Includes: Fair Pay, Three Meals a Day, Safety, Work-Life Balance (stability)
    • Leadership read: High concentration = survival needs dominate; bandwidth for engagement may be limited.
    • Signals: turnover tied to pay, fatigue call-outs, safety incidents, overtime stress
    🤝 Quadrant II — Belonging & Team Connection
    “Do I feel part of something here?”
    • Includes: Teamwork, Communication, Kindness & Compassion, Belonging
    • Leadership read: High concentration = team climate matters; communication breakdowns will hit hard.
    • Signals: interdepartment friction, handoff failures, peer conflict, low pre-shift engagement
    🚀 Quadrant III — Personal Growth & Future Orientation
    “Is there a future for me here?”
    • Includes: Growth & Opportunity, Leadership Support (development), Work-Life Balance (growth)
    • Leadership read: High concentration = workforce is ready for pathways, training, internal mobility.
    • Signals: training participation, cross-training interest, promotion readiness, career talks
    🏆 Quadrant IV — Respect, Voice & Emotional Intelligence
    “Do leaders truly see and value me?”
    • Includes: Respect & Appreciation, Recognition, Leadership Support (dignity)
    • Leadership read: High concentration = dignity signals are the leverage point; leadership behavior is being evaluated.
    • Signals: grievance tone, supervisor feedback, discretionary effort, QA attentiveness, guest empathy behaviors
    Why this matters: The quadrant model separates baseline stability needs from higher-order dignity signals, enabling more precise and emotionally intelligent intervention design.

    Quadrant Framework for Workforce Value Mapping

    We consolidate the 12 values into 4 quadrants so leaders can translate sentiment into action — and track it over time.

    🧭 Quadrant I — Foundational Security

    Stability
    • Values: Fair Pay, Three Meals a Day, Safety, Work-Life Balance
    • Signal: “Do I feel materially safe and supported enough to do my job?”
    • Leadership focus: reduce stressors, improve predictability, make safety visible

    🤝 Quadrant II — Belonging & Team Connection

    Culture
    • Values: Teamwork, Communication, Kindness & Compassion, Belonging
    • Signal: “Do I feel part of something here?”
    • Leadership focus: strengthen handoffs, pre-shifts, and team rituals

    🚀 Quadrant III — Personal Growth & Future Orientation

    Pathways
    • Values: Growth & Opportunity, Leadership Support, Work-Life Balance
    • Signal: “Is there a future for me here?”
    • Leadership focus: training, cross-training, clear advancement criteria

    🏆 Quadrant IV — Respect, Voice & Emotional Intelligence

    Dignity
    • Values: Respect & Appreciation, Recognition, Leadership Support
    • Signal: “Do the people in charge truly see and value me?”
    • Leadership focus: supervisor tone, follow-through, “You said / We did”

    Why this matters

    This model distinguishes baseline stability needs from higher-order dignity signals — so interventions are more precise, emotionally intelligent, and operationally measurable.
